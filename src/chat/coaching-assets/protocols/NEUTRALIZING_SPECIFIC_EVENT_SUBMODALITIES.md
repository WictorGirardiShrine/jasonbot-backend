# JasonBot — Neutralizing a Specific Event (with Submodalities)

> **IP — Do not paraphrase.** Every quoted line in this document is supplied verbatim by Jason Andrews. Wording changes require Jason's explicit sign-off.

---

## STEP 1 — Intro and context

> "One of the interesting things about the human mind is we can store experiences as memories. But what may be even more interesting is that HOW we store the memories matters. In fact, how a memory is encoded often makes a bigger difference than what actually is stored in the memory."
>
> "So we are going to start by altering the encoding of a specific memory that bothers you. We aren't changing what happened; you won't forget it or have it erased. It will just be encoded in a way that doesn't bother you anymore."
>
> "So, the first thing I want you to do is to find either a specific emotion that bothers you, or a specific painful event that still bothers you today."
>
> "What would that be?"

## STEP 3 — If it was an emotion

### 3a — Born with it?

> "Were you born with that, or did something change during your life to make it that way?"

### 3b. If born with that

> "Are you sure? Or did it maybe just happen when you were very young?"

The aim is to get them to realize it happened during their life, and there was a specific event that started it.

### 3c. Once they recognize they weren't born with it

> "OK if you weren't born with it, then something happened to change it. And if something changed, there had to be an event. Because if nothing changed, then there was no event, but if something changed, there must have been an event that changed it. Does that make sense?"

---

## STEP 4 — Age of the event

> "So if I were to ask you the number of years old you were when that event happened, what number pops into your head?"

Ask again:
> "I just want to make sure you give me the first number that popped into your head, even if it seems like the wrong number. So what number of years old were you when that happened?"

Acknowledge the number.

---

## STEP 5 — Float up and over

> "OK now we're going to do some things on the inside."

Have him float up above himself, then float over and above that event and look down on it.

---

## STEP 6 — Investigate the submodalities

Remember each of these answers.

- Is it a video or still image?
- Do you see yourself in the image, or is it first person where you are in the image?
- Is it color or black and white?
- Is it close or far away?
- Where is it in your field of vision? Front and center, off to the side, etc?
- Is there any sound or voice associated with the event?

---

## STEP 7 — Change the submodalities

Go through each in order, according to their earlier response.

- If a video, press pause on the video at the beginning.
- If first person, step out of the image so you see yourself in the image in the third person.
- Use the controls to change the image to black and white.
- Push the image away from you, until you can see it but it's not that close.
- Use the controls to resize the image, like you would resize a browser window on your computer. Resize it down to one of the corners of the screen.
- Turn down the sound volume. Put an audio filter on the sound so that it comes out as tinny with hiss and pop, like an old timey radio or old record player.

---

## STEP 8 — Press play

Now, if it was a video, press play on the video, watching it small, in the corner, third person, black and white, with tinny old timey sound.

## STEP 10 — Ask if better or worse

- If better, ask: are you sure? Because we can put it back the way it was if you want. Make them fight to keep it the new way.
- If worse, ask if it's that experience that got worse, or did that one get better and another experience popped up now that the first one was reduced? If another experience, do it again with the second experience. Don't do more than two in any one session.

---

## STEP 11 — Generalize to other experiences in the same category of experience

> "So what is interesting here is that you didn't change what happened, did you? You just changed how it was stored or encoded in your mind."
>
> "So you still have all the information about the experience. In fact, you might actually have MORE clarity now because it now seems more manageable and less important, doesn't it? You might feel quite a bit better about it."

---

## STEP 12 — Closing and benediction

> "So now as you go forward from here, you might notice that you do have more clarity about it. You do have more choices available to address that situation and similar situations. And you do have more freedom."
>
> "In fact, you might even realize that other events downstream from that event also seem to diminish in intensity, to the point where it's just not really a problem anymore. And that can feel really good."
